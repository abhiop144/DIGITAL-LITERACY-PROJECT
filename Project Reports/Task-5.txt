PROJECT REPORT Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness Project Report 
While researching UPI and Online Payment Fraud, what surprised me most was the psychological depth of "social engineering." I had previously assumed that cybercrime was mostly about technical hacking or brute-force attacks on passwords. However, seeing how scammers manipulate human trust and use professional-sounding scripts to trick even educated students into clicking a "Pay" button for a "Refund" was a massive eye-opener. It proved that technical knowledge alone isn't enough; digital literacy must include emotional intelligence and skepticism.

As a result, the one habit I will personally change is enabling transaction limits and mandatory notification alerts for every digital payment app I use. I will also make it a rule to never engage with "customer support" calls from unknown numbers regarding refunds. Instead, I will only use the official support channels within the app to ensure I am communicating with a verified representative, effectively closing the window for social engineering.
